Mar 03

Kyoto, Japan

— temples, tea & bamboo forests —

Fushimi Inari at dawn ♥

Walked through the thousand vermillion torii gates at sunrise — the light filtering through was absolutely magical. I could hear nothing but birdsong and my own footsteps on the gravel path.

The bamboo grove in Arashiyama made me feel so small (in a good way). Had the best matcha of my life at a tiny tea house near Kinkaku-ji. overpriced tourist trap Actually worth every yen.

Must come back during cherry blossom season!!

my rating: ★★★★★
try the street food in Nishiki Market!!
→ octopus on a stick = amazing

Apr 12

Santorini, Greece

— sunsets, blue domes & white walls —

Oia sunset — no filter needed

Every single building is a postcard. The white-and-blue palette against the deep Aegean sea is almost too beautiful to be real. Watched the sunset from Oia and literally everyone around me started clapping.

Ate fresh grilled octopus by the water in Ammoudi Bay. Tried the local Assyrtiko wine — crisp, volcanic, perfect. The caldera views from our little cave hotel were unreal.

Don't need to see another sunset ever again … who am I kidding, I watched it every single night.

my rating: ★★★★★
Note to self:
bring more sunscreen next time.
SO much sun.

Jun 28

Patagonia, Argentina

— glaciers, peaks & endless wind —

Torres del Paine at golden hour

The Torres del Paine massif is hands-down the most dramatic landscape I have ever seen. Granite spires rising out of turquoise lakes, glaciers calving into the water with thunderous cracks. I cried a little. (Don’t tell anyone.)

Did the W Trek over 5 days. My legs have never been so sore. Easy hike, very relaxing Every muscle in my body is screaming, but my soul is full. The Perito Moreno glacier is a wall of impossible blue ice.

Patagonia wind is NO JOKE. Lost my hat three times.

my rating: ★★★★☆
packing list:
- windproof jacket!!!
- better hiking boots
- snacks (so many snacks)

Sep 05

Marrakech, Morocco

— souks, spices & hidden riads —

the medina at golden hour

The Jemaa el-Fnaa square at night is pure sensory overload — snake charmers, storytellers, the smell of grilled lamb and cumin drifting from food stalls. I got lost in the souks approximately 47 times (not exaggerating).

Our riad had a courtyard with a fountain and orange trees. Waking up to the sound of the call to prayer echoing across the medina rooftops was hauntingly beautiful. Took a cooking class and learned to make the best tagine of my life.

Haggling tip: start at 1/3 the price. Walk away. They always call you back.

my rating: ★★★★★
bought:
- leather bag (gorgeous)
- 3 lanterns
- too many spices
stayed on budget

Nov 19

Reykjavík, Iceland

— northern lights, hot springs & lava fields —

Aurora borealis!!! I screamed.

Saw the Northern Lights on our second night and I am not the same person anymore. Green and purple curtains rippling across the sky like someone was painting the heavens in real time. No photo does it justice. None.

Drove the Golden Circle — Gullfoss waterfall is so powerful you can feel the ground trembling. Soaked in the Blue Lagoon while snow fell on our heads. It was a little cold It was FREEZING but absolutely magical. Also: Icelandic hot dogs are unreasonably good.

The silence out in the countryside is unlike anything I've ever experienced.

my rating: ★★★★★
LAYERS. Wear
all the layers.
thermal underwear
is not optional.

Jan 14

Amalfi Coast, Italy

— limoncello, cliff roads & la dolce vita —

Positano from the water — a dream

Positano tumbles down the cliff face like a cascade of pastel confetti. Pink, peach, terracotta, lemon yellow — every building looks like it was painted by someone deeply in love. Took a boat along the coast and saw it all from the water. Perfection.

Ate the best pasta of my entire life at a tiny family restaurant in Ravello — handmade scialatielli with fresh clams, a glass of local Falanghina, lemon cake for dessert. The grandmother came out of the kitchen and pinched my cheeks. Peak Italy.

Diet starts Monday The Path of the Gods hike was incredible — cliffside trails with views that made my heart stop (both from beauty and from fear of heights).

I left my heart on the Amalfi Coast. Going back for it someday.

my rating: ★★★★★

day 1: 2
day 2: 3
day 3: 4
day 4: ...lost count

Feb 18

Prague, Czech Republic

— spires, cobblestones & Czech beer —

Charles Bridge at sunrise — pure magic

The Astronomical Clock struck the hour and all the little apostle figures came out — surrounded by tourists with their phones up, but somehow it still felt enchanting. This city is an open-air museum that somehow also has the best beer I’ve ever tasted.

Wandered through the Old Town at dusk. The Gothic spires against the pink sky looked like something from a fairy tale. Crossed the Charles Bridge with the Vltava glittering below. Baroque, Gothic, Art Nouveau — every street corner is a different century.

Just one more beer Lost count at a tiny pub in Mala Strana. Czech lager is dangerously drinkable. Also tried trdelnik — the cinnamon pastry thing — and I may be addicted.

The silence inside St. Vitus Cathedral made me hold my breath.

my rating: ★★★★★
beer prices:
- pint = ~$2 !!!
- at a tourist spot = $4
- still cheaper than
  literally anywhere else

Mar 22

Machu Picchu, Peru

— ruins, mist & the Inca Trail —

the citadel emerging from the clouds

Four days on the Inca Trail and then suddenly — there it was. The clouds parted like a curtain and Machu Picchu appeared below, terraces cascading down the ridge, mountains rising on every side. I actually gasped out loud.

The stonework is insane — no mortar, and you can’t fit a piece of paper between the blocks. The Incas were on another level. Sat on a terrace in absolute silence and just… existed. The altitude made me dizzy but my heart was so full it didn’t matter.

Easy four-day hike, no big deal My knees are filing a formal complaint. Dead Woman’s Pass nearly lived up to its name. But worth every single step.

Coca tea is a lifesaver at altitude. Drink ALL the coca tea.

my rating: ★★★★★
packing essentials:
- altitude sickness pills
- rain poncho (clouds = wet)
- good hiking poles
- emotional readiness
  to ugly-cry at beauty
