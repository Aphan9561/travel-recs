A Very Tilted Guide to the

Best Places to Travel

( nothing here is straight — and that’s the point )

Kyoto

Japan — East Asia

Ancient temples draped in moss, bamboo forests that whisper secrets, and golden pavilions reflected in mirror-still ponds. A city where tradition is woven into every cobblestone.

temples tea ceremonies cherry blossoms zen gardens

Santorini

Greece — Mediterranean

White-washed walls tumbling toward an impossibly blue sea, sunsets that set the sky on fire, and volcanic wine sipped on cliffside terraces. Every corner is a postcard.

sunsets blue domes caldera views wine

Patagonia

Argentina & Chile — South America

Granite spires punching through clouds, glaciers that crack like thunder, and windswept plains stretching to the edge of the world. Raw, untamed, and utterly humbling.

glaciers hiking Torres del Paine wild

Marrakech

Morocco — North Africa

A maze of souks overflowing with spices and lanterns, riads hiding tranquil courtyards, and a main square buzzing with storytellers and sizzling street food after dark.

souks spices riads tagine

Reykjavík

Iceland — North Atlantic

Northern lights painting the sky in emerald ribbons, geothermal pools steaming under snowfall, and lava fields that look like another planet. Silence so deep you can hear the earth breathe.

aurora hot springs glaciers volcanoes

Amalfi Coast

Italy — Southern Europe

Pastel villages cascading down sea cliffs, the scent of lemon groves drifting on warm air, handmade pasta by the water, and winding coastal roads with views that steal your breath.

limoncello cliffs pasta la dolce vita

Prague

Czech Republic — Central Europe

A fairy-tale city of Gothic spires and Baroque palaces, where cobblestone lanes wind past astronomical clocks, and the Vltava River reflects centuries of art, music, and revolution.

architecture history beer old town

Machu Picchu

Peru — South America

The lost citadel of the Inca, perched among the clouds on a razor-thin ridge, where ancient stone terraces cascade into mist and the silence holds the memory of an empire.

ruins trekking history Inca trail
